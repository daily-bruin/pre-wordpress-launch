Want to be more involved?

We're accepting applications this summer for an internship.

If accepted for this internship, you will help our web producer develop and launch the new Daily Bruin website. The site currently runs on a CMS (content management system) that offers very little flexibility. By Fall 2012, we plan to introduce a custom-built in Wordpress site. This is a substantial undertaking that involves a full-scale redesign: reorganizing the pages, adding new features, creating modules that emulate the features on the current site, and more. For that reason, we are seeking interns with skills in design and web development.

The internship will also extend to creating online projects, such as covering presidential and state elections or on-campus events over the summer.

About

About the online department

The online department is new at the Bruin: summer 2012 will be the first quarter we will be accepting interns. It has two goals:

  1. To keep dailybruin.com and our blogs working well, looking great, and up to date. The online department is responsible for uploading content to the website and helping every section in the newsroom take advantage of the site. For instance, we are currently working on improving radio and video integration with the site.
  2. To use the internet as a medium for reporting. We are journalists, but instead of writing stories to publish in a paper, we write code for users to interact with. This type of storytelling is perfect for displaying complex data or immersing readers in video or audio elements that can only be shown online.
